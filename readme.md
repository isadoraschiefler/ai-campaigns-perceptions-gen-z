# ai-campaigns-perceptions-gen-z
Dataset from a study investigating how Generation Z young adults perceive authenticity, trust, and visual experience in AI-mediated brand campaigns compared to human-produced campaigns.

AI-Mediated Brand Campaigns — Dataset Overview

This repository contains the dataset and supporting materials from a quantitative study investigating how Generation Z young adults perceive authenticity, trust, and visual experience in brand campaigns mediated by Artificial Intelligence (AI), compared to human-produced campaigns.

The study was conducted with 150 participants aged 18–28 residing in Florianópolis, Brazil, using structured survey instruments and non-parametric statistical analysis.

Research Scope

The dataset focuses on:

Perceived authenticity (human vs AI-generated campaigns)
Brand trust in AI-mediated communication
Evaluation of visual experience
Interaction preferences (human vs AI-driven)
Comparative perception of visual stimuli

The study is limited to the aesthetic dimension of brand communication, excluding other AI applications.

Data Description

The dataset includes:

Sociodemographic variables
Exposure to AI-mediated campaigns
Likert-type scale responses (agreement and evaluation)
Comparative ratings between human-produced and AI-generated images
Categorical variables on interaction preference and trust

All variables are structured for statistical analysis and replication.

Methodological Notes
Data collected via online questionnaire (Google Forms)
Measurement scales: five-point Likert-type and comparative rating scales
Sample size: 150 respondents
Analysis performed using Jamovi (version 2.6)
Statistical methods include:
Wilcoxon signed-rank test
One-sample Wilcoxon test
Chi-square goodness-of-fit test
Key Findings (Summary)
Human-produced campaigns are perceived as significantly more authentic than AI-mediated campaigns
AI-mediated campaigns are associated with lower levels of perceived trust
Visual experience evaluations for AI content are moderately negative
A strong preference for human-centered interaction was observed
